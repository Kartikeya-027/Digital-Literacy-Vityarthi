Prevention Checklist: Stay Safe Online

Account & Password Security

* Use unique passwords for every account. Never reuse passwords across your college email, media and banking apps. A password manager like Bitwarden can help you generate and store them.

* Enable two-factor authentication on all accounts. Turn it on for Gmail, Instagram and your banking/UPI apps. Use an authenticator app like Google Authenticator or Microsoft Authenticator. Don't just rely on SMS as SIM-swap fraud is common in India.

UPI & Financial Safety

* Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. Your PIN is only needed to send money. If someone asks for your PIN or sends a request saying it will transfer money to you it's a scam. Decline immediately. Block the contact.

* Always verify the recipients name before every UPI transfer. Confirm the name the app shows after you enter a UPI ID. Scammers use IDs like refund@okaxis or support@ybl to appear legitimate. Set a transaction limit in your bank app and enable SMS/email alerts for all payments.

Phishing & Scam Awareness

* Never click links in WhatsApp, SMS or emails from senders. Fake scholarship offers, part-time job scams and fake KYC update alerts are common. Always go directly to the website by typing the URL yourself.

* Never call customer care numbers found on Google. Scammers pay to rank helpline numbers above real ones. Use the number printed on your debit card or inside the app. If support staff asks you to install AnyDesk or TeamViewer hang up. It's a scam.

Social Media & Privacy

* Set all social media profiles to private. Avoid publicly sharing your location hostel address, college ID, date of birth or Aadhaar details. Cybercriminals use profiles to craft targeted attacks against you.

* Beware of friendship or romance accounts. If someone you met online asks for money, gift cards or intimate photos stop all contact. Sextortion is a crime. Don't pay; report it immediately.

Network Security

* Never do banking or UPI transactions on Wi-Fi. Hostel common room networks, café Wi-Fi and open college networks can be monitored. If unavoidable use a free VPN like Proton VPN to encrypt your connection.

* Only install apps from the Play Store or App Store. Never install APK files shared via WhatsApp or Telegram. This is how malware spreads on Android phones. Keep your OS and apps updated for the security patches.

If You're a Victim. Report Immediately

Time is critical. Reporting within the hour increases the chance of recovering money and catching the criminal.

* National Cyber Crime Portal: Visit cybercrime.gov.in to file complaints for UPI fraud, sextortion, social media abuse and more.

* Cyber Crime Helpline: Call 1930. This is a 24×7 service available in Hindi & English. For fraud call immediately to freeze the transaction.

* Police Emergency: Call 112 for physical safety situations.

Project Report: Reflection

What surprised me most while researching this topic was the sophistication of Digital Arrest scams. I was shocked to learn how fraudsters impersonate ranking police officials over video calls to keep victims under arrest virtually for hours. They coerce victims into transferring their savings. It highlighted that cybercrime in India has moved beyond technical glitches to advanced emotional manipulation.

As a result of this research one habit I will personally change is my use of SMS, for two-factor authentication. I previously assumed SMS was secure. Learning about SIM-swap fraud and notification mirroring malware has convinced me to switch to App-based Authenticators like Google Authenticator. This small change ensures that even if my phone number is compromised my primary accounts remain protected by a device-linked token.

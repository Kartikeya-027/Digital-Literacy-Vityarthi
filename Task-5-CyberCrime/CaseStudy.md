Case Study: The Social Sabotage Campaign

Fictional scenario based on documented patterns of online harassment in India.

What is it?

Cyberbullying is when people use media, messaging apps and online gaming to hurt, threaten or embarrass someone. This kind of bullying follows the person into their spaces can be seen by thousands right away and often leaves a lasting online record thats hard to get rid of.

How it typically happens:

The step-by-step plan of this scam:

* Targeting and Exclusion: A group of people former friends or classmates make a secret chat to plan a coordinated attack on the person.

* Fabrication: They create media or rumors to damage the persons reputation.

* Coordinated Harassment: The group launches a raid on the persons profiles flooding comments with abusive language and encouraging strangers to join in.

* Doxing: The persons info, like their home address or phone number is shared online to move the harassment from the internet to real-life intimidation.

Who is targeted?

Teenagers students and young adults are often targeted. People from marginalized communities or those who seem different within a circle are frequently singled out. However with people depending on the internet professionals and public figures are increasingly facing coordinated campaigns to cancel or troll them.

Consequences

For the person being targeted the consequences include emotional trauma, anxiety, depression and in extreme cases self-harm. At school or work victims often face isolation and a big drop, in performance.

In India cyberbullying can be prosecuted under sections of the Information Technology (IT) Act, 2000:

* Section 66E: Violating someones privacy.

* Section 67: Sharing material.

* Section 509 of the BNS ( IPC): Actions meant to insult a womans modesty.

Punishments can include imprisonment ranging from 3 to 5 years and significant fines.

Preventive tip:

Never engage with or retaliate against a bully; this often fuels their behaviour. Instead document everything by taking screenshots use Block and Report tools away and keep social media profiles private to control who can interact with you.

Source:

National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (cybercrime.gov.in); UNICEF India End Violence digital safety guidelines; CyberPeace Foundation reports.

Responsible Social Media Use — Guidelines for College Students

Recommended Practices

Exercise discretion before posting — carefully consider the potential long-term impact of your content, particularly on academic and professional opportunities.
Review and manage privacy settings — ensure that your personal information and posts are accessible only to appropriate audiences.
Verify information prior to sharing — rely on credible and authentic sources to prevent the spread of misinformation.
Maintain respectful communication — engage in constructive discussions and express disagreements with courtesy and professionalism.
Regulate screen time — allocate sufficient time for academic responsibilities, rest, and personal well-being.
Report inappropriate content — promptly use platform mechanisms to address harassment, abuse, or misleading information.

Practices to Avoid

Avoid disclosing sensitive personal information — refrain from sharing details such as your address, contact number, or daily routine publicly.
Do not engage in cyberbullying — any form of online harassment or intimidation is unethical and may have serious consequences.
Refrain from posting impulsively — avoid sharing content in moments of anger or emotional distress; thoughtful reflection is essential.
Do not share others’ content without consent — respect privacy and intellectual property rights at all times.
Avoid unhealthy comparisons — recognize that social media often portrays idealized versions of reality.
Do not reuse passwords across platforms — ensure strong and unique passwords to maintain account security.
